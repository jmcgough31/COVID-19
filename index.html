A definite pattern has emerged in the new daily cases. as the previous 3 weeks, the beginning of the week shows decline then as the week progresses the cases rise. Last week was no different. With a steady rise up to the weekend then a decline in new cases. over the last 4 weeks the average daily new cases have shown a decline. the data shows we are on the downward side of the curve. However, its much less steep than the climb up.

The U.S. deaths show a similar patter although not as consistent. On Saturday and Sunday, the daily death growth rate was lower than the daily new cases growth rate, the first time ever. Resulting in a case fatality rate .04% less than Thursday. If this trend remains the U.S. case fatality rate will continue to decline.

Virginia Is still trending upward for new cases. with its largest number of new cases to date on Friday (1,055) with only a small decline on Saturday (836) and similar numbers on Sunday (934). Daily growth rate has been stuck around 5% for the last 10 days with little movement beyond +- 1%. until the data is showing a consistent downward daily growth percentage. Making predications based on the data will be difficult. With that said It seems like northern VA could be on the decline while other large cities like Richmond are on the rise. I still think mid to end of May for the V.A. peak overall.

Mid May will be an interesting time for data. This is 14 days after many of the states have started opening back up. It will be interesting to see if there is an increase in daily cases/deaths.
